# CS 330 Portfolio Reflection

## How do I approach designing software?

Throughout this course, I learned that successful software design begins with planning before writing code. For my final project, I selected a music and vinyl listening area because it contained recognizable objects that could be recreated using basic geometric shapes. I broke the overall scene into smaller objects, such as the record player, speakers, shelving, chair, and decorations, and planned how each would be built before implementing it. This process made the project more manageable and allowed me to make improvements throughout development.

### What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?

This project strengthened my ability to analyze a real-world scene and recreate it using simple 3D objects. I also gained experience with arranging objects, applying textures, selecting lighting, and positioning a camera to create a visually appealing environment. These skills helped me think more carefully about how design choices affect the overall appearance of a project.

### What design process did you follow for your project work?

I followed an iterative design process by first creating the basic shapes for each object, then adding transformations, textures, lighting, and camera controls. At each milestone, I reviewed my progress, tested the scene, and made adjustments until the environment more closely matched my original design.

### How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?

Breaking larger projects into smaller, manageable tasks is a strategy that I can apply to future software projects. Planning the overall design first and then implementing features one step at a time makes development more organized and reduces the likelihood of becoming overwhelmed.

## How do I approach developing programs?

I approach software development by focusing on one feature at a time while testing frequently to make sure each change works before moving on. This helps me identify problems early and makes debugging much easier than trying to solve multiple issues at once.

### What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?

One of the biggest strategies I learned was building complex objects from simple geometric primitives. I also gained experience organizing code into reusable functions, applying textures and lighting, and making small incremental improvements instead of attempting to complete everything at once.

### How did iteration factor into your development?

Iteration was an important part of this project. After completing each milestone, I tested the scene, identified areas that could be improved, and refined my work. This included adjusting object placement, improving textures, modifying lighting, and refining camera movement until the final scene achieved the appearance I wanted.

### How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones, which led you to the project's completion?

As I progressed through the milestones, I became more confident reading and modifying larger amounts of C++ code. I also became more comfortable debugging graphics-related issues and understanding how different parts of the program worked together. By the final project, I was able to make changes more efficiently and with greater confidence than I could at the beginning of the course.

## How can computer science help me in reaching my goals?

Computer science provides me with the technical skills needed to pursue a career in software engineering. Each project I complete strengthens my programming, problem-solving, and critical thinking abilities while also expanding my portfolio with examples of my work.

### How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge and skills that can be applied in your future educational pathway?

This course introduced me to concepts such as geometric modeling, transformations, textures, lighting, cameras, and collision detection. These topics expanded my understanding of computer graphics and gave me practical experience applying mathematical concepts within software development.

### How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge and skills that can be applied in your future professional pathway?

Although I plan to pursue a career in software engineering with a focus on backend development, this course gave me valuable experience working with larger C++ projects, debugging complex programs, and understanding how graphics applications are developed. The problem-solving skills, structured development process, and iterative approach I practiced throughout this course will benefit me in future software engineering roles, even outside of graphics programming.
